Chapter 2 Data sources

The primary data source we used for this project is the Economic Research Service of the U.S.Department of Agriculture *(ERS)*. ERS makes estimates of the milk production costs by using the dairy version of the data from the Agricultural Resource Management Survey (ARMS).The estimates the ERS made are based both on the actual data gathered from the ARMS and the estimating models. ERS updated the estimates twice a year, one in May and one in October. October released the final version. The estimates is annual milk cost of production estimates by State and by size of the operation. The Milk Cost of Production Estimates data is better used with non-analytical purposes. More information and background of the data can be found at this webpage. All members of our team are responsible for collecting the data. The data is collected by directly download the .xlsx file from the official webpage of the Economic Research Service. While we are deciding which dataset is better to illstate our purposes, we also considered another data set called U.S. Food Imports. There is a dairy section in the data set. However, we finally decided that the milk production data can better express the milk related problem in the United States rather than the import activity. More details about the data set we used for this project are as follows:

Issues with this data set:

Even though this data set is well organized. It still has some issues.

  1. The data value is not accurate enough to be used for analysis purposes.By reading the data set background on Economic Research Service’s official website, it is easier to find that some amount of the data is estimated by using actual data and model. It makes sense that it is difficult to ask and gather milk production and seeling data from farmers, because it requires lots of work, and farmers may even do not know exactly how much they paid or gained in the milk production industry.

  2. The data set only covers milk production data of 19 States of the United States. It could be that the amount of milk production and seeling can be ignored compared to these 19 States. However, it is imappropreate to just treat the milk production of the other States as “NO MILK PRODUCED” at all.
